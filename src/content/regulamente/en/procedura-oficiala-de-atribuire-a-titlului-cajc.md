__1. Classes eligible for CAJC__

CAJC may be awarded __only in the following classes__:

- __Very Young (9–12 months)__
- __Young (12–18 months)__

⚠ *The Baby and Puppy classes are NOT eligible for CAJC.*

__2. Minimum conditions for awarding CAJC__

A dog may receive CAJC __only if__:

__✔ it has obtained the grade Excellent 1 in its class__

(“…that have obtained the grade of Excellent 1…” – Art. 8)

__✔ it belongs to the official WDF list of breeds__

(“…belonging to the official list of canine breeds approved by WDF.”)

__✔ it is the best specimen of its sex in the Junior / Very Junior class__

__3. Number of CAJCs awarded per breed__

The judge may award:

- __1 CAJC to the best male__ (from Very Young + Young)
- __1 CAJC to the best female__ (from Very Young + Young)

*Without distinction of varieties.*  
(E.g.: for breeds with varieties – short/long hair – only one CAJC/sex is awarded for the entire breed.)

__4. The concrete awarding procedure in the ring__

__🔹 Step 1: The judge assesses each dog in the Very Young and Young classes__

→ awards the individual grades.

__🔹 Step 2: The judge places the dogs graded at least Very Good__

→ but CAJC may be awarded __only__ to specimens with __Excellent 1__.

__🔹 Step 3: The final comparison is made between:__

- the winner of the Very Young MALE class
- the winner of the Young MALE class

→ the winner of the duel receives __CAJC Male__.

The same for __females__.

__5. Approval of CAJC by the secretariat__

Article 8 clearly provides:

__✔ CAJC is approved on the same day by the secretariat__

→ before the end of the show

__✔ it is recorded in the WDF Qualification Booklet__

__6. The qualification booklet requirement__

If a dog __does not have a qualification booklet__, even if it is a winner:

__❌ it cannot collect CAJC__

__✔ CAJC is transferred to the next dog placed (with Excellent 2)__

__7. CAJC is at the judge’s discretion__

The regulations specify:

“The awarding of CAJC remains at the discretion and judgment of the expert judge…”

So the judge:

- __is not obliged__ to award CAJC
- even if eligible dogs are present
- if the judge considers that no dog deserves the distinction

__8. Conditions for the final “Junior Champion” title__

In accordance with Art. 39:

- It is obtained upon the accumulation of __4 CAJC__,
- from __at least 3 different judges__.

__📌 OPERATIONAL SUMMARY FOR JUDGES – CAJC__

1️⃣ The Very Young & Young classes are judged (males/females).  
2️⃣ Individual grades are awarded.  
3️⃣ Only dogs with __Excellent 1__ are eligible.  
4️⃣ The winners of the two classes of the same sex are compared.  
5️⃣ The judge decides at discretion who receives the title.  
6️⃣ The title is approved by the secretariat.  
7️⃣ It is recorded in the qualification booklet.  
8️⃣ No booklet → CAJC passes to the next placed dog with Excellent 2.
